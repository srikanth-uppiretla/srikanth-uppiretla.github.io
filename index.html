About Me

Design intent first, geometry second.

I am a mechanical engineer based in Bremen, Germany, currently completing my Master's degree at the University of Bremen. My thesis work at ZARM (Center of Applied Space Technology and Microgravity) combines hands-on hardware development with analysis — an environment where a design is only finished once it survives the load case.

In parallel, I design mechanical components and multi-part assemblies in CATIA V5: parametric part modelling, fully constrained assemblies, and production-ready 2D drawings to DIN/ISO standards. Every project on this page follows the same discipline — a stated design intent, clean feature trees, and a drawing package at the end.

I am currently looking for engineering roles in design and development in Germany.

Structural Analysis Work

Analysis conducted as part of my Master's thesis at the Center of Applied Space Technology and Microgravity (ZARM). The workflow below reflects how I approach every structural problem — the setup and interpretation matter more than the contour plot.

01

Problem definition & load cases

Requirements and load cases derived from the mission environment — quasi-static launch loads and the relevant safety factors.

02

Idealization & boundary conditions

Geometry simplification, material properties, contact and constraint definition — documented so the model is reproducible.

03

Meshing & convergence

Element selection and local refinement at critical features, with a mesh convergence check before trusting any result.

04

Results → design decisions

Margins of safety against yield at critical locations, and the concrete design changes the analysis drove.
